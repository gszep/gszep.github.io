# Research notes: "If I don't do it, someone else will."

Source material and editorial decisions for the essay at [`src/content/blog/en/if-i-dont-do-it-someone-else-will.mdx`](../../src/content/blog/en/if-i-dont-do-it-someone-else-will.mdx).

## Context

Three-week business trip to Silicon Valley from Tokyo. Meetings with MATS (Ryan Kidd), Google (Blaise Aguera y Arcas, Shane Gu), Softmax (Adam Goldstein). Two phrases kept surfacing across rooms:

1. *"If I don't do it, someone else will."*
2. *"Make sure you're on the right side of the chasm when takeoff begins."*

The essay argues these are load-bearing rationalisations — fixed points of an arms-race equilibrium — and that no amount of inside-steering dislodges them. The call to action: organise / strike / build exogenous institutions. Closing frame: dramatic silence.

### ABS formula derivation (verified from first principles)

Expected utility for team $i$:

$$\mathbb{E}[U_i] = \mathbb{P}(i\text{ wins})\,s_i + (1-e)\sum_{j \neq i} \mathbb{P}(j\text{ wins})\,s_j$$

Derivation: condition on who wins.
- If $i$ wins, $i$ gets $s_i \cdot 1 + (1-s_i) \cdot 0 = s_i$ (safe win pays 1, disaster pays 0).
- If $j \neq i$ wins, $i$ gets $s_j \cdot (1-e) + (1-s_j) \cdot 0 = (1-e)\,s_j$.
- Sum weighted by win probabilities. $(1-e)$ factors out since it doesn't depend on $j$.

Assumes disaster pays 0 uniformly. Variants use $-d$; qualitative result unchanged.

Closed-form symmetric Nash equilibrium exists only under specific distributional assumptions (Gaussian skill noise, symmetric teams). Essay's interactive plot uses the stylised tractable form

$$s^*(N, e) = \frac{1}{1 + (N-1) \cdot e \cdot 3}$$

to reproduce the qualitative collapse without pretending to derive the paper's formula.

### Slider design decisions

- **$N$ (teams)** default = 5 (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google DeepMind, xAI, Meta — US frontier cluster). Alt counts: 7 adds DeepSeek, Alibaba Qwen; 3 = top tier only.
- **$e$ (enmity)** floor raised from 0 to 0.1 — no zero-competition state is credible.
- **$\mathbb{P}(\text{doom})$** range 0.1%–50% covers range of published expert estimates.
- **$\mu$ (marginal per researcher-year)** floor raised from $10^{-7}$ to $10^{-6}$ so prose claim "even at the low end, the gap doesn't close" survives the slider's extreme. At P(doom)=0.001, μ=10⁻⁷, social cost = $8M < $10M private (1.25× smaller). At μ=10⁻⁶, social = $80M (8× private).
